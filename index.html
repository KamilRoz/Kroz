Why Moving to Sheffield Feels Like the Best Decision I Ever Made!

Posted on 20th April 2025

When I first moved to Sheffield, I expected a typical city—busy streets, a buzzing high-energy vibe, and maybe a park or two. What I got was so much better. Sheffield isn’t just a city; it’s a gateway to the outdoors, with the Peak District right on its doorstep (seriously, you can hike epic trails and still be back in time for coffee). But what really won me over was the way nature weaves into everyday life here—think lunch breaks in the Botanical Gardens, sunset walks at Endcliffe Park, or stumbling across hidden green spaces between the indie shops and cosy pubs. And yet, it’s far from sleepy.

The city thrums with live music, quirky cafes, and a proper Northern warmth that makes strangers feel like neighbours. If you love the idea of a place where adventure and urban life actually get along, Sheffield might just steal your heart like it did mine.

Job Hunting in Sheffield: The Reality of Setbacks and Small Wins

Posted on 30th April 2025

Let’s be honest—moving to Sheffield hasn’t been the smooth transition I’d hoped for, at least not career-wise. Despite polishing my CV, tailoring cover letters, and applying relentlessly, landing interviews has been tougher than expected. Rejections (or worse, silence) started to feel routine, and there were days when I questioned whether I’d ever break into the local job market.

But Sheffield has a way of keeping you going. The city itself—with its affordable rent, green spaces, and laid-back charm—makes the struggle a little easier to bear. I’ve found comfort in its community, too; chatting with other job seekers over coffee or stumbling across networking events reminded me I’m not alone in this.

I’m still searching, still sending out applications, and still holding onto hope. Because if there’s one thing Sheffield teaches you, it’s resilience. And when that right opportunity finally comes, it’ll be worth the wait.

Finding Home in Hillsborough: Why This Neighbourhood Stole My Heart

Posted on 19th May 2025

After months of flat hunting across Sheffield, I’ll admit I was starting to lose hope—until I stumbled upon the perfect place in Hillsborough. Nestled just northwest of the city centre, this area somehow manages to feel like a proper community while keeping urban convenience close by. My new home is a cosy Victorian terrace with just enough quirks to give it character, and the location couldn’t be better.

Hillsborough itself surprised me. I’d heard about the famous Wednesday stadium, but I wasn’t prepared for the lively high street packed with butchers, bakeries, and the kind of independent cafes where baristas remember your order. The tram whizzing into town takes 15 minutes, but most days I’d rather wander the side streets or picnic in Hillsborough Park—especially now the rhododendrons are in bloom.

What really won me over, though, was the neighbourhood vibe. Chatty post-office queues, dog walkers who nod hello, and a proper local pub that hasn’t been gentrified beyond recognition. It’s the sort of place where you quickly stop saying “my house” and start saying “my street”—and after all those viewings, that’s exactly what I needed.

New Beginnings: Upskilling with CodeYourFuture and Construction Training

Posted on 5th June 2025

Exciting news—I’ve just enrolled in a part-time course with CodeYourFuture, a fantastic charity helping people break into tech! As someone eager to pivot into web development, I can’t wait to dive into their project-based learning and supportive community. Balancing this with another big commitment: next Monday, I start a fast-paced Intro to Construction course, which will wrap up with me earning my CSCS Green Card—the golden ticket to site work—after just two weeks!

It’s going to be intense juggling both, but I’m determined to make it work. CodeYourFuture’s flexible evenings-and-weekends schedule fits perfectly alongside the construction training, and I love that both paths open doors to in-demand careers. By the end of this, I’ll have coding skills and practical construction knowledge—maybe even options I hadn’t considered before.

Right now, it’s all about showing up, staying curious, and trusting the process. Here’s to new skills (and maybe a few hard hats in my future)!
