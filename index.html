Salmon and Dill Pasta

  • Time: 15min
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Fresh or Dried Tagliatelle 320g
Philadephia Cream Cheese 180g
Semi-Skimmed Milk 2tbsp
Fresh Lemon Juice 10ml
Smoked Salmon 125g
Fresh Dill 100g
Black Pepper To taste

Step 1: Bring a large pan of salted water to the boil and cook the pasta as directed on the pack.

Step 2:Meanwhile, spoon the Philadelphia into a pan with the milk. Stir over a gentle heat until the Philadelphia has melted. Stir in the lemon juice.

Step 3:Drain the pasta, reserving a little of the cooking water.

Step 4:Toss the pasta in the Philadelphia sauce, adding a splash of the cooking water to loosen. Stir in the smoked salmon and dill. Add black pepper to taste. Serve immediately.

Source: Philadelphia website

Chicken Casserole

  • Time: 50mins
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Bone-in chicken thighs 8
Oil 1tbsp
Spring Onions 5
Plain Flour 2tbsp
Chicken Stock Cubes 2
Large Carrot 2
New Potatoes 400g
Frozen Peas 200g
Grainy Mustard 1tbsp
Parsley Small handful

Step 1:Put the kettle on. Fry 8 bone-in chicken thighs in 1 tbsp oil in a casserole dish or wide pan with a lid to quickly brown.

Step 2:Stir in the whites of 5 spring onions with 2 tbsp plain flour and 2 chicken stock cubes until the flour disappears, then gradually stir in 750ml hot water from the kettle.

Step 3:Throw in 2 large carrots, in batons and 400g new potatoes, bring to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20 mins.

Step 4:Take off the lid and simmer for 15 mins more, then throw in 200g peas for another 5 mins.

Step 5:Season, stir in 1 tbsp grainy mustard, the green spring onion bits, a small handful of fresh soft herbs and some seasoning.

Source: BBC Good Food website

Cottage Pie

  • Time: 1h 10mins
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Olive Oil 1tbsp
Beef mince 500g
Onion 1
Carrot 2
Celery Stick 2
Plain Flour 1tbsp
Dried Thyme 1tsp
Dried Sage 1tsp
Beef Stock 500ml
Tomato Puree 2tbsp
Worcester Sauce 2tbsp
Salt and Pepper To taste
White Potatoes 1kg
Salted Butter 2tbsp

Step 1:Preheat your oven to 180℃/350F, 200℃/425F or Gas mark 6.

Step 2:Heat your olive oil in a large, heavy bottomed pan. Add in the mince and fry for 5 minutes on a high heat until browned all over.

Step 3:Add in the chopped vegetables and fry for a further 10 minutes until softened.

Step 4:Add flour and dried herbs, stir well to coat everything, cook for a further 2-3 minutes.

Step 5:Pour in beef stock, tomato puree and Worcester sauce.

Step 6:Season with salt and pepper. This is personal preference but I like to add lots of both in this recipe.

Step 7:Cover and simmer for 30 mins.

Step 8:Meanwhile peel and chop your potatoes, place in cold water, and bring to the boil for 15-20 minutes, depending on the type of potato.

Step 9:When the potatoes are ready, drain and mash together with the butter and plenty of salt and pepper.

Step 10:Put all of your meat into a large ovenproof dish. Spread your mashed potato over the top, ruffle with a fork to give those delicious crunchy bits. (You can also sprinkle some grated cheese on top if you wish).

Step 11:(At this point you can leave to cool and then freeze your pie for up to one month to get ahead with your cooking).

Step 12:Bake in the oven for 25-30 minutes until the mash is browning and the mince is bubbling at the sides.

Step 13:Check that it is hot inside. It may take a few minutes longer if you cooked it straight from the fridge.
